About

Environmental science, research, teaching, and community impact.

Shamina Israt Tithi is an Earth and Environmental Sciences educator and researcher based in Queens, New York. She holds a Master of Arts in Earth and Environmental Sciences from Brooklyn College, CUNY, and a Bachelor of Science in Forestry from the University of Chittagong.

Her academic and professional work connects environmental science, sustainability, GIS, soil research, forestry, climate resilience, and community engagement. As an Adjunct Lecturer at Brooklyn College, she teaches undergraduate students about dynamic Earth processes while designing lectures, assignments, interactive labs, and real-world learning activities.

Her experience also includes sustainability coordination, environmental field inspections, seafood processing recovery research, soil profile analysis, disaster risk reduction planning, climate-resilient community projects, and biodiversity-focused forestry research.
